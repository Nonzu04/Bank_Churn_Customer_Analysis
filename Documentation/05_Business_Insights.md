# Bank Customer Churn Analysis - Business Insights

## Executive Summary

This report summarizes the key findings from the exploratory data analysis of the bank customer churn dataset.

The analysis identified demographic and behavioural factors associated with customer churn and highlights customer segments that may require targeted retention strategies.

Overall, approximately **20%** of customers have exited the bank, indicating that customer retention remains an important business challenge.

---

# Key Findings

## 1. Overall Customer Churn

### Finding

- Overall customer churn rate: **20.37%**
- Approximately one in every five customers has left the bank.

### Business Insight

The bank retains most of its customers; however, a churn rate above 20% suggests opportunities to improve customer retention and loyalty through proactive engagement.

---

## 2. Customer Age

### Finding

| Age Group | Churn Rate |
|-----------|-----------:|
| Young Adult | 9.97% |
| Middle Age | 37.09% |
| Senior | 27.79% |

### Business Insight

Middle-aged customers recorded the highest churn rate.

This customer segment may have changing financial needs or greater exposure to competing banking products. Targeted retention campaigns and personalised financial services could reduce customer attrition.

---

## 3. Gender

### Finding

| Gender | Churn Rate |
|---------|-----------:|
| Male | 16.46% |
| Female | 25.07% |

### Business Insight

Female customers were more likely to leave the bank than male customers.

Further investigation into customer satisfaction and product usage may help explain this difference.

---

## 4. Geography

### Finding

| Country | Churn Rate |
|----------|-----------:|
| Germany | 32.44% |
| France | 16.15% |
| Spain | 16.67% |

### Business Insight

Germany recorded the highest churn rate.

Regional customer experience, competition, or product offerings should be investigated to understand why customers in Germany are leaving at a significantly higher rate.

---

## 5. Customer Tenure

### Finding

| Tenure Group | Churn Rate |
|--------------|-----------:|
| New Customer | 21.15% |
| Established Customer | 20.64% |
| Loyal Customer | 19.51% |

### Business Insight

Customer churn decreases slightly as customer tenure increases.

Retention programmes should focus on strengthening customer relationships during the early years.

---

## 6. Account Balance

### Finding

| Balance Group | Churn Rate |
|---------------|-----------:|
| Low Balance | 14.25% |
| Medium Balance | 19.88% |
| High Balance | 25.23% |

### Business Insight

Customers with high account balances recorded the highest churn rate.

Since these customers represent greater financial value to the bank, retaining them should be considered a strategic priority.

---

## 7. Active Membership

### Finding

| Active Member | Churn Rate |
|---------------|-----------:|
| Yes | 14.27% |
| No | 26.85% |

### Business Insight

Inactive customers were nearly twice as likely to leave the bank.

Increasing customer engagement through digital banking, rewards programmes, or personalised communication may reduce churn.

---

## 8. Number of Products

### Finding

| Products | Churn Rate |
|-----------|-----------:|
| One Product | 27.71% |
| Two Products | 7.58% |
| Multiple Products | 85.89% |

### Business Insight

Customers with two products demonstrated the strongest retention.

Conversely, customers with multiple products experienced extremely high churn rates, suggesting this segment requires further investigation to determine the underlying causes.

---

# Advanced Customer Segmentation

## Age Group × Active Membership

### Finding

Senior inactive customers recorded the highest churn rate (**84.82%**).

### Business Insight

This segment represents the highest-risk customer group identified during the analysis.

Retention initiatives should prioritise inactive senior customers before account closure occurs.

---

## Geography × Gender

### Finding

Female customers in Germany recorded the highest churn rate (**37.55%**) among all gender-country combinations.

### Business Insight

Regional and demographic-specific retention campaigns may improve customer loyalty within this segment.

---

## Credit Score × Active Membership

### Finding

Inactive customers consistently recorded higher churn rates across every credit score category.

### Business Insight

Customer engagement appears to be a stronger predictor of churn than credit score alone.

---

## Number of Products × Geography

### Finding

Customers with multiple products in Germany recorded the highest churn rate (**91.67%**).

### Business Insight

This represents the highest-risk product-geography segment and should be prioritised for further business investigation.

---

# Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended:

- Develop targeted retention campaigns for middle-aged customers.
- Improve customer engagement among inactive members.
- Investigate the causes of high churn within the German market.
- Strengthen onboarding programmes for new customers.
- Prioritise retention of high-value customers with large account balances.
- Investigate why customers holding multiple banking products are leaving at unusually high rates.
- Use predictive analytics to identify customers at high risk of churn before they exit.

---

# Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates that customer churn is influenced by multiple demographic and behavioural characteristics rather than a single factor.

Customer activity, geography, product ownership, and age emerged as the strongest indicators of churn.

The insights generated from this project provide a foundation for data-driven customer retention strategies and can support future predictive modelling and business intelligence initiatives.
